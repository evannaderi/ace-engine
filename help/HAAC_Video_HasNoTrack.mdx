### Verify if media embedded with a `<video>` element provides text alternatives for audio or visual content using a `<track>` element or open captions

<div id="locLevel"></div>

Media embedded with a `<video>` element must provide text alternatives for audio or visual content using a `<track>` element or open captions

</Column>
</Row>
<Row>
<Column colLg={11} colMd={5} colSm={4} className="toolMain">

### Why is this important?

Text tracks or open captions contain a textual transcription of either audio (captions) or visual (visual/video description) aspects of video or audio content in a synchronized media. This may include the dialog, sound effects, and relevant musical cues, or information about characters, location, actions and scene changes. Providing text alternatives makes information available that some people would not have access to otherwise.

<div id="locSnippet"></div>

### What to do

* Use the `<track>` element with the `kind` attribute to provide text tracks for the audio (`kind="captions"`) and visual (`kind="descriptions"`) aspects of the video;
* OR make sure the video has no audio;
* OR make sure the video has open captions, usually done by professional real-time captioners (also called Communication Access Realtime Translation (CART) providers).

For example:

<CodeSnippet type="multi" light={true}>&lt;video src="..."&gt;
      &lt;track src="myvideo.srt" kind="captions" &gt;
&lt;/video&gt;</CodeSnippet>

</Column>
<Column colLg={5} colMd={3} colSm={4} className="toolLeft">

### About this requirement

[IBM 1.2.1 Audio-only and Video-only (Prerecorded)](http://www.ibm.com/able/guidelines/ci162/audio_and_video.html)
[WCAG 2.1 technique H96](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/Techniques/html/H96)

### Who does this affect?

* Blind people using screen readers
* People who are deaf or hard of hearing
